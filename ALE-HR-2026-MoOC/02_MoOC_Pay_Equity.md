## Change Description

### What will change?
ALE will implement a governance structure for pay equity and execute corrective compensation adjustments to mitigate labor and reputational risks. This involves:
- Establishing a consistent pay equity analysis methodology across all BUs and terminals
- Identifying and quantifying gender and role-based pay gaps
- Designing and executing phased corrective actions (adjustments to individual compensation)
- Aligning ALE's compensation practices with Glencore global standards and Brazilian labor regulations
- Strengthening transparency and internal fairness in pay decisions

### How and when will the change be implemented?
The change will be implemented in phases due to budget constraints:

- **Phase 2:** *[For Larissa to Complete]*
- **Phase 3:** *[For Larissa to Complete]*

Key milestones:
1. Obtain external legal advice on pay equity exposure - *[For Larissa to Complete - target date]*
2. Complete pay equity analysis (linked to Salary Benchmarking project) - *[For Larissa to Complete - target date]*
3. Create corrective action plan with costing - *[For Larissa to Complete - target date]*
4. Secure RemCo approval - *[For Larissa to Complete - target date]*
5. Execute Phase 1 corrections - *[For Larissa to Complete - target date]*

### What is the objective of the change?
- Eliminate unjustified pay disparities across gender, role, and tenure dimensions
- Mitigate legal risk under Brazilian labor law (CLT and equal pay legislation)
- Reduce reputational risk and align with Glencore's commitment to fair pay
- Establish ongoing governance to prevent future pay equity drift

### How was the change designed?
- Design informed by external legal advice *[For Larissa to Complete - confirm legal firm engaged]*
- Salary Benchmarking project (led by Taiane) provides market data foundation
- Phased approach designed to manage budget impact across fiscal periods

### How is the organisation changed and affected?
- Individual employees will receive compensation adjustments where gaps are identified
- Managers will receive updated compensation guidelines and pay equity governance requirements
- HR will take on ongoing monitoring and reporting responsibilities

### How are overall roles, responsibilities, and accountabilities to change?
- **GM HR:** Responsible for pay equity governance framework and annual reporting
- **HR Business Partners/Managers:** Responsible for applying equity guidelines in all compensation decisions
- **BU GMs:** Accountable for equity within their business units
- **Finance/CFO:** Budget approval for corrective adjustments
- *[For Larissa to Complete - Additional role changes]*

### Risk areas
- **People:** Employee expectations may be raised; those not receiving adjustments may feel unfairly treated. Risk of constructive dismissal claims if handled poorly.
- **Legal/Compliance:** Brazilian labor law requires equal pay for equal work (Lei 14.611/2023). Non-compliance carries fines and reputational damage.
- **Financial:** Budget impact of corrections must be managed across phases.
- **Contractors:** *[For Larissa to Complete - Are contractor pay rates in scope?]*
