## Change Description

### What will change?
ALE will implement an HR governance and process excellence model covering:
- **Standardization:** Uniform HR processes across all BUs and terminals (recruitment, onboarding, performance management, offboarding, etc.)
- **Compliance:** Alignment with ALE's TSA authority matrix (Sections 13.1-13.6) and Glencore global standards
- **Digitalization:** Assessment and potential implementation of new HR systems (linked to HR Systems Assessment project - payroll replacement/outsourcing, Performance Management System)
- **Efficiency:** Elimination of manual/duplicated processes; streamlined workflows

### How and when will the change be implemented?
*[For Larissa to Complete - Provide phased approach:]*
- Phase 1: Process mapping and gap analysis (current vs. desired state)
- Phase 2: Governance framework design and approval
- Phase 3: System selection and implementation (if applicable)
- Phase 4: Rollout, training, and monitoring

### What is the objective of the change?
- Ensure consistent application of HR policies and procedures across ALE
- Achieve full compliance with the TSA authority matrix for all HR decisions
- Reduce manual effort and error rates through digitalization
- Create auditable HR processes that withstand internal and external scrutiny
- Align ALE's HR operations with Glencore global standards

### How was the change designed?
*[For Larissa to Complete - Describe approach: Gap analysis against TSA requirements? Benchmarking against Glencore best practices? Current pain points identified?]*

### How is the organisation changed and affected?
- **HR team:** New processes, potential new systems, additional governance responsibilities
- **Line managers:** More structured HR processes to follow; clearer authority levels per TSA
- **All employees:** Impacted by digitalized processes (e.g., self-service portals, electronic approvals)
- **Finance:** Impacted if payroll system changes; closer integration with HR governance

### How are overall roles, responsibilities, and accountabilities to change?
Per the TSA authority matrix, roles are already defined. This initiative ensures they are consistently applied:
- **GM HR:** Overall governance ownership; Recommend/Agree/Input roles per TSA Sections 13.1-13.6
- **BU GMs:** Decision authority per TSA for their business units
- **CFO:** Agree/Recommend role per TSA for compensation and organizational changes
- **CEO/BoD:** Final decision authority per TSA
- *[For Larissa to Complete - Are any new roles being created (e.g., HR Systems Administrator, Compliance Officer)?]*

### Risk areas
- **People:** Process changes may face resistance from managers used to informal approaches; HR team needs upskilling on new systems
- **Compliance:** Transition period where old and new processes coexist creates compliance risk
- **Technology:** System implementation carries data migration, integration, and adoption risks
- **Contractors:** *[For Larissa to Complete - Are external HR/payroll contractors affected?]*
